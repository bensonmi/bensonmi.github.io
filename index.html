Professional Self-Assessment

My time in the Computer Science program has been instrumental in shaping my professional goals and values, preparing me to be a valuable asset to prospective employers. This ePortfolio showcases my ability to plan and develop industry-standard code and software programs. Although this is not a complete reflection of my work in this program, it effectively depicts my knowledge in Software Design and Engineering, Algorithms and Data Structures, and Databases.

In addition to my academic experience, I have worked for 26 years in an industrial plant, where my main focus was PLC programming and HMI (Ignition and IFIX). My extensive experience in this industry has provided me with a background in Python, C++ (DOS), Visual Basic, structured text, Ladder Logic, and some vision systems.

Collaborating in a Team Environment:
Throughout my years in IT and SNHU Computer Science program, I engaged in multiple team projects. These experiences taught me the importance of collaboration and how to leverage the strengths of team members to produce a cohesive product.

Communicating with stakeholders:
 My communication skills already had from my work experience were honed through courses at SNHU which required interaction with stakeholders to ensure project requirements were met. Many of the courses focused on SCRUM where I learned to communicate effectively with various roles in a team, including QA, product owners, and project managers.

Data structures and algorithms:
 My understanding of data structures and algorithms was improved going though Full-stack Devopement

Software engineering and database:
 I have used many database in my current career but in SNHU i got to use MongoDB which added to my skills in databases

Security:
Security has been a significant part of my studies. SNHU provided me with knowledge on creating secure software and understanding vulnerabilities. In my projects, I implemented security measures such as input validation and encryption to protect data.
